LLMs are Maya Maxxing


Dharma is increasingly important in the era of AI


Language as a proxy for the mind

Alan Turing’s imitation game[1] made language the original proxy for artificial intelligence. This served as the foundation of the Turing Test (which is the new name of the test[2], and the popular Cumberbatch biographical film). While some argue that language and AI are synonymous, there are strong arguments against it (the “stochastic parrot”). Language can often give us the perception of intelligence. It can trick us into thinking that the entity is more intelligent than it actually is.[3]

On the other hand, language can influence thought. One of my favorite Jordan Peterson arguments against Canada’s Bill C-16 was that “legislating language is legislating thought”. The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis (linguistic relativity[4]) makes a deeper claim about cognition and worldviews being impacted by the language we surround ourselves with[5].

This importance of language was well understood by our ancestors. It is Lindy. Religions put a premium on the words we say, the hymns we chant, the mantras we repeat. In the Sikh faith, language is the vehicle to Liberation.


Dharma

The foundation of most Dharmic schools of thought[6] is that there is only One True existence or reality. This is typically known as Brahman (not Brahma) or Waheguru[7], depending on the tradition. The physical world is a projection of Brahman. One way to think about this is that it is a 2D circular projection of a 3D sphere. It’s “real”, but not complete[8].

Most Dharmic faiths say that we are trapped in this 2D projection. We are trapped due to Maya. This is the 2D paper referenced above. We cling to this paper, and we cling to being within this circle, because it is visible and obvious to us. But this clinging keeps us stuck to the 2D paper for lifetimes[9].

The goal of Dharma is to liberate ourselves (mukti, moksha) from the 2D paper and into the 3D spherical space (Waheguru / Brahman). And the way to do this is to fight against the bondage[10] imposed on us by Maya.


Maya

This is a fascinating topic. Every Dharmic tradition has their own take on it. It deserves a full, judgment-free, write up in the future. For now, I focus on the parts that help me build my thesis.

For simplicity’s sake, let’s take the extreme position that Maya is bad. It’s an illusion, tricking us to think that the 2D paper we exist on is the only reality. And this illusion is reinforced by the Five Thieves[11]:

* Attachment

* Lust

* Wrath

* Greed

* Ego or excessive focus on the Self


The Five Thieves glue us to the paper inscribed by the circle. They strengthen the sheet, thicken the circle’s borders and perforate us into the paper itself.

In Dharmic faiths, this is horrifying. The Ultimate Sin[12]. You want to do anything you can to minimize the impact of these thieves, so that you don’t get sucked deeper into the paper. And if you don’t succeed, that means you remain trapped in this 2D plane for this life. You will have to try again.[13]


Maya Maxxing

One tension between the Dharmic and Abrahamic faiths[14] is the role of desire in the afterlife. The Islamic afterlife, specifically, is a beautiful and vibrant world where all your materialistic desires are fulfilled[15]. However, from the Dharmic perspective, Jannah is Maya maxxing. It strengthens your ties to the Five Thieves and thus traps you on the 2D plane forever.


LLM Heaven

And this is where LLMs are especially deadly for our atman[16]. We are in unprecedented territory. We can now create our own linguistic Mayapic heavens.

LLMs have two ingredients that can be devastating for our future. They can present a world custom-designed to root us into Maya. And they can convince us that they are intelligent enough to trust.

Of course, this is not a new problem. In the last century, modern life has increasingly trained us to worship measurement: KPIs, metric, optimization, engagement, productivity, growth. This is Maya Maxing. The observable reality (2D projection) becomes the focus. LLMs are the natural evolution of this system.
